From an Apprentice's Wrench to a Trusted Business

My name is Mike Hargrove, and I've been a plumber my whole adult life. I grew up in Columbus watching my father fix things with his hands — he always said "if something's broken, learn how to fix it." That stuck with me.

"I started this company because I was tired of seeing homeowners get overcharged and underserved. You deserve to know exactly what you're paying for — and to have it done right."

I started as an apprentice at 18, sweating pipes in attics during Ohio summers and crawling under houses in January. I earned my journeyman license at 22 and my master plumber's license at 26. By 2003, I had saved enough to buy one van, a set of tools, and a dream.

I started ClearFlow Plumbing out of that single van with a simple promise: honest pricing, quality work, and showing up when we say we will. Word spread fast in the neighborhood, and within two years I had to hire my first employee — my brother-in-law, Dave.

Today, ClearFlow has grown to a team of 14 licensed plumbers, two office staff, and a fleet of fully-stocked service vans. We've completed over 1,500 jobs across central Ohio. But our core promise hasn't changed one bit.
